Justify Items Pitfall

Justifying items is a very nice approach especially in the context of responsive design. In the given examples the classes justify-wrapper and justify-item are the essentials. container and box are just a little layout. Just have a look at the source code for details. But this approach has a big pitfall: All justified items have to been separated by a line-break. If they were not, they will not be aligned correctly.

1.) Separate justified items with line-breaks: works!

<div class="container justify-wrapper">
    <div class="box justify-item"></div>
    <div class="box justify-item"></div>
    <div class="box justify-item"></div>
    <div class="box justify-item"></div>
</div>

2.) Do NOT separate justified items with line-breaks: doesn't work!

<div class="container justify-wrapper"><div class="box justify-item"></div><div class="box justify-item"></div><div class="box justify-item"></div><div class="box justify-item"></div></div>

3.) Separate justified items with html entity '&#10;' (line feed): works!

<div class="container justify-wrapper"><div class="box justify-item"></div>&#10;<div class="box justify-item"></div>&#10;<div class="box justify-item"></div>&#10;<div class="box justify-item"></div></div>
